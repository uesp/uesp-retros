Memorial

Unfortunately we lost several prominent members of the Elder Scrolls community in 2025 as listed below.

    Julian LeFay - 22nd July 2025

    Elder Scrolls co-creator Julian LeFay, the core game director, programmer, and designer at Bethesda Softworks in the 1980s and '90s, was considered the chief imagineer behind The Elder Scrolls series during his tenure of the studio, being the project director behind Arena, Daggerfall, and Battlespire, contributing to the programming, gameplay mechanics, and lore design of the series.
    〉

    Laralyn McWilliams - 8th February 2025

    Laralyn was an Italian-born American game designer and producer and was a former member of the Council of Wisdom and helped beta test The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall before its release in 1996.
    〉

    Jim Ward - 10th December 2025

    Jim was the talented voice actor and radio personality who voiced Mannimarco in The Elder Scrolls Online main quest. He previously retired in 2021 due to health issues, as he was diagnosed with both COVID-19 and Alzheimer's, leaving him unable to continue voice acting. Ward had roles in other Bethesda games such as Dr. Klein in Fallout: New Vegas and Old Longfellow in Fallout 4. He was also known for his roles in many cartoons and games from the 2000s, such as Doug Dimmadome & Chet Ubetcha in The Fairly OddParents, Captain Qwark in the Ratchet & Clank franchise, Perceptor in the Transformers: War for Cybertron games, Professor X in Wolverine and the X-Men, and Diamondhead & XLR8 in Ben 10.
    〉

    Terence Stamp - 17th August 2025

    Terence was a veteran English actor with a career in film spanning six decades, and voiced Mankar Camoran, one of the main antagonists in Oblivion. He was also known for his role as General Zod in Superman, and as a favor to Todd Howard said "Kneel before Todd" (seen in the Making of Oblivion Documentary) in the same tone as his iconic line from the film.
    〉

    Renée Victor - 31st May 2025

    Renée was the voice actress for female Argonians in Skyrim and female Khajiit in Oblivion Remastered.
    〉

    Noah Nelson - 1st June 2025

    Noah was the voice actor for male orcs in Skyrim who also provided voicing for male orcs in Oblivion Remastered. He passed away during the development of OBR and the game is dedicated to him.
    〉

Major Events

27 January 2025

Wes Johnson in Intensive Care

Wes was sent to intensive care unit after being found unresponsive. He has since recovered and has been released from hospital.

Wes has played characters in every TES game from Morrowind to Elder Scrolls Online, including the mobile spinoffs Legends and Blades. He's most famous for being the voice of Sheogorath in Shivering Isles and Skyrim, but he has also played roles as diverse as Emperor Titus Mede II, the Arena Announcer, Lucien Lachance, all Imperial males in Oblivion, and all Breton and Orc males in Morrowind. In The Elder Scrolls Online he provided the voices for Ard Caddach and Hermaeus Mora. Outside of video games, he is the Announcer for the NHL team Washington Capitals.

30 January 2025

ES Legends Shutdown

The servers for the Elder Scrolls card game Legends have been permanently shut down on January 30, 2025 at 10am EST. We at UESP have had a great time with the game and bid it a heartfelt farewell. We are still busy documenting and archiving the game. There are still many things that can be done after the shut down. We have back-ups of the game files, many many screenshots, and recollections of the players that can be used to fill in the last bits of information to preserve the game to the fullest. If you want to help, head over the Legends Namespace Completion Project.

8th October 2025

ES6 NPC Project

In the spring of 2025 Bethesda announced a charity auction with the prize being the chance to design a custom NPC for Elder Scrolls 6. The UESP gathered over a 100 people from the Elder Scrolls and gaming community in order to pool our money and make a bid but unfortunately the auction went a "bit" higher than we expected ($85,000!) which was outside of our available funds at the time.

Fortunately the story didn't end there....Bethesda reached out to us after the event and made us an offer. If we were able to match the final auction amount they would give us a chance to create another custom NPC for the Elder Scrolls 6. A group of more than 50 of us pooled our money and were able to reach the goal of $85,000. We spent the summer creating a very detailed design document for the NPC and in the fall presented it to a group of Bethesda developers in a virtual meeting.

We can't say more than that at the moment but are excited for the time when we can talk more about it as well as let you meet/experience the NPC yourself!

7th November 2025

30th UESP Anniversary

Hello everyone, we are proud to announce that this month marks the 30th Anniversary of the UESP! For three decades now, the Unofficial Elder Scrolls Pages has helped to keep the Elder Scrolls community up-to-date with all the latest information and contribute to the documentation of the franchise, first as an FAQ that began in the autumn of 1995, and later as a wiki starting in 2005. It has continued to grow and evolve since then, now standing at over 115,000 articles!

We would like to give a large thank you to all the editors and readers who have contributed so much to this project and made the site what it is today. We really don't know what else to say, since the club of sites that have been around that long is very, very small. Our gratitude towards everyone in the community cannot be stated enough!

Thank you for letting us be your source for The Elder Scrolls since 1995 BL-icon-Health.png Here's to many more years to come!

One of the primary challenges this year was continuing to fight unruly bots who would either perform too many requests in a short period or try to scrape content that was database or CPU heavy and would eventually cause a partial or full site outage. Almost all outages in 2025 were due to bots scraping things they shouldn't be accessing or scraping content without regard to server speed.

In 2025 we've migrated all domains to be under CloudFlare with its advanced AI and bot detection and banned capabilities which is a lot easier than try to do server side IP bans like we were doing in 2024. Some bot attacks comprise an absolutely huge number of IPs sometimes in the 100s of thousands which was impossible to handle with individual IP bans. CloudFlare also lets us do a Managed Challenge rather than an outright ban so that a real person that might happen to be caught can still access the site.

This year we will try to improve the bot detection and management to be more proactive and do some server side changes to try and limit any badly behaving bot (or user) that might happen to get past the CloudFlare defenses.
